28th September — 1st October 2023

The Independent Manchester Beer Convention (Indy Man Beer Con) is a world-class showcase for the best breweries from the UK and beyond. Taking over the stunning surrounds of the Grade II listed, architectural gem Victoria Baths, IMBC brings together the most progressive, forward-thinking breweries around for a multisensory, headlong, hop-forward beer extravaganza.

Brought to you by the team behind Common, Port Street Beer House, The Beagle & Nell's, IMBC was inspired by the UK craft beer movement and frustration that beer festivals in the early days weren’t really representative of this exciting and dynamic part of the industry. We wanted to put on an event that has amazing beer (obviously) but that was also open-minded, inclusive and modern.

Indy Man Beer Con is hosted in the glorious surroundings of Victoria Baths. Opened in 1906, the venue was origninally described as "the most splendid municipal bathing institution in the country" and "a water palace of which every citizen of Manchester can be proud".

Today, the building still boasts many of its original features, including glorious stained-glass windows, terracotta tiles, and a beautiful array of mosaic floors.

Travel

Victoria Baths is just a short journey from Manchester City Center. There are a few ways you can get there.

By Bus:
The nearest bus route is the number 50, which runs from Manchester City Centre (Princess Street) to East Didsbury. Get off at the junction of Upper Brook Street, Anson Road and Hathersage Road, and walk east along Hathersage Road to the Baths (1/4 mile).

By Taxi:
A Taxi from Piccadilly Station shoud take less than 10 minutes and cost around £8.

By Car:Be advised that there is very limited parking during the event. Also, please don't drink and drive.

Accessibility

There is ramped access to the main public entrance of the Baths. Within the building, the ground floor is fairly level with ramped access into the sports hall. There are quite a few floors with uneven surfaces.

There are no lifts within the building but all of the main spaces can be visited or viewed from the ground floor.

There is adequate disabled parking to the rear, the building has a fully-accessible toilet, and during the event all three main rooms will be accessible, alongside the Turkish Baths and outdoor areas.

Kids & Dogs

Children are very welcome to attend IMBC before 7pm on any given session accompanied by a guardian. Dog-wise, guide dogs are welcome but the rest of our canine friends have to stay at home.

Victoria Baths is an old building with lots of nooks and crannies. It will also most likely be a busy, noisy environment and we don’t want any dogs to get trodden on or scared. Same goes for kids so keep ’em close!
